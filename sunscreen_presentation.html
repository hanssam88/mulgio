Executive Summary

5 Key Findings

Critical insights for strategic decision-making

  1. US market is ~77x larger than Korea

    Korea ~$150M vs US ~$11.5B in sunscreen-only segment (2024), presenting massive TAM opportunity

  2. Regulatory divergence creates structural barrier

    Korea classifies sunscreen as cosmetics; US requires OTC drug approval via FDA monograph

  3. Korea became #1 sunscreen exporter to US in 2024

    Surpassing France, driven by K-beauty demand and superior formulation technology

  4. Ingredient innovation gap favors Korea

    16+ modern UV filters approved in Korea vs only 17 (many outdated) in the US

  5. Distribution channel strategy differs fundamentally

    Korea: Olive Young (H&B) dominance; US: Walmart/Target mass + Amazon DTC growing rapidly

!
K-beauty sunscreen has a structural edge in formulation but faces high regulatory and distribution barriers in the US market.

Risk Assessment

Key Risks & Mitigation

FDA Regulatory Delay High

Mitigation: Begin FDA OTC monograph compliance in Phase 1; engage US regulatory counsel early; consider formulating with existing GRASE-approved ingredients only for initial US launch.

US Distribution Lock-out High

Mitigation: Build Amazon #1 position first as leverage for retail buyer meetings; secure Sephora "K-beauty sunscreen" shelf before mass retail approach.

Korean Market Saturation Medium

Mitigation: Differentiate via unique IP (proprietary filter combinations); focus on underserved sub-segments (men's, sensitive skin, baby).

Supply Chain Disruption Medium

Mitigation: Dual-source critical UV filter ingredients; maintain 90-day safety stock; establish secondary OEM relationship.

IP / Counterfeit Risk Medium

Mitigation: Register trademarks in US/Korea before launch; implement anti-counterfeit packaging; monitor Amazon 3P sellers proactively.

Currency / FX Volatility Low

Mitigation: Price in local currency; hedge 6-month forward contracts on major USD/KRW exposure above $500K.

!
FDA compliance and US distribution access are the two highest-impact risks; both require early investment in Phase 1.
